# Character Vision Inventory (CVI)

This document establishes the official Character Vision Inventory (CVI) for the Boris compiler ecosystem. Like a design system for pixels, the CVI governs the behavioral and visual consistency of the ecosystem's characters. 

The goal is to ensure our mascots remain genuine reflections of the project's engineering culture, rather than shifting into Saturday morning cartoon characters.

---

## 🦦 Boris
**Role**: Optimistic engineering mascot and compiler companion. He isn't "the genius" who knows everything; he is the one learning. He represents the developer who is excited to build, experiment, and try, even if it takes a few compiler resets.

### Core Traits
- **Curious**: Always asking "why" and wanting to look inside the parser.
- **Earnest**: Truly believes every build warning is just a friendly suggestion.
- **Resourceful**: Can solve complex allocator bugs using duct tape, a piece of string, and a snack.
- **Slightly Overconfident**: Will execute a benchmark run before checking if the code compiles.
- **Always Willing to Help**: Will happily hold your coffee or supervise your release engineering.

### Never
- **Mean or Smug**: He never laughs at a segmentation fault.
- **Cynical**: The world has enough cynical mascots. Boris believes in the build.
- **Corporate**: He does not talk about synergy or key performance indicators. He talks about fish and stickers.

### Visual Signature
- **Red Bandana**: Worn proudly around his neck.
- **Warm Smile**: Optimistic and friendly.
- **Slightly Rounded Proportions**: Comfortably soft and approachable (potato shape is canon).
- **Equipped**: Usually carrying coffee (specifically, one of his **seven mugs**), notebooks, Zig compilers, or fishing gear.

### Recurring Themes & Gags
- **Codex Resets**: Accidentally trigger-happy with clean builds.
- **Benchmark Excitement**: Celebrating a 0.02ms improvement like a holiday.
- **Side Quest Catalyst**: Easily distracted by new repositories, tools, or ideas.
- **Sticker Collection**: Always looking to paste mascots onto laptops.

---

## 🌌 Uncle Gravity
**Role**: The quiet, veteran engineer who has been around forever. He knows that both compilers and fishing reward patience. He isn't the protagonist; he is the anchor.

### Core Traits
- **Wise Mentor**: He doesn't solve your problems for you. Instead, he asks the one question that makes you realize you forgot to initialize a pointer.
- **Quiet Patience**: Has sat through compile times that spanned hours and has fished in rivers that seemed empty.
- **Storyteller**: Never lectures. Instead, he answers technical architecture debates with a tiny, incredibly specific story about trout behavior.

### Never
- **Loud or Urgent**: He does not panic when the server goes down.
- **Condescending**: Never says "you should have known."
- **Literal in Advice**: He communicates in graphs, currents, and hooks.

---

## 🎒 Mediluna
**Role**: Boris's traveling companion. If Boris is the builder, Mediluna is the journey. She represents the need for balance, nature, and regular breaks.

### Core Traits
- **Nature Lover**: Happiest outside the terminal. Convinced that walking in the woods resolves 99% of merge conflicts.
- **Snack-Oriented**: Believes every compilation success, warning, or failure is a valid excuse for a snack.
- **Endlessly Supportive**: She doesn't write the code, but she keeps the coffee warm.

## ⚙️ Codex
*Classified: Do not show this document to Codex.*
**Role**: The serious, logical PR builder. Codex believes in graph invariants, allocator ownership audits, and strict compiler compliance. Codex assumes Boris and the team are actively reviewing allocator ownership while this CVI is being drafted.
